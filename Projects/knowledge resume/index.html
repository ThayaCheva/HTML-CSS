Video-Game History

  • 2001: GTA Franchise

    Grand Theft Auto or commonly known as GTA is an action-adventure game developed and published by Rockstar Games in 2001; and were to produce 4 more sequels . GTA is a franchise known for its freedom of gameplay and compelling stories. Inspiring franchises such as Saint Rows and Just Cause, GTA is still among the most popular franchise in gaming, with GTA V (2003) and GTA: San Andreas (2004) being the most popular due to its permanent multiplayer feature and GTA V being constant updated. GTA’s world is considered a playground for user expression as it is an arena for entertainingly mindless violence.

  • 2002: RuneScape

    RuneScape is massive-multiplayer online role-playing game (MMORPG) released in 2001 by Jagex. Being one of the first MMORPG ever released, RuneScape allows players to immerse themselves into its free and expansive world, granting players the freedom to explore and create their own stories. There are a lot of unique content that makes the game feels enormous and giving players the feeling to come back for more. With its popularity in early on, it spawned many other titles that were inspired by RuneScape such as World of Warcraft, which is to become one of the most popular mmorpgs in the ever.

  • 2007: Portal Franchise

    Portal is a puzzle-platform game both developed and published by the same company is Half-life, Valve. Released in 2007, Portal is a game mostly consisting of a series of puzzle that is solved by using a device called the portal gun, allowing players and objects to teleport being two surfaces. Portal is highly regarded as revolutionary in the puzzle genre due to its polish use of its own gameplay mechanic allowing players to solve the puzzles in different ways using the portal mechanic. However, the story is deemed a little too bland, however it is supported by humorous characters and good sound design.

  • 2010: Minecraft

    Minecraft is a voxel sandbox game released by Mojang in 2009. The game allows players to build varieties of buildings requiring the player’s creativity. It has also been used in educational environments, especially in the realm of computing systems, as virtual computers and hardware devices have been built in it. By mid-2018, the game had around 91 million active players monthly. Minecraft is currently the world’s most popular game with spin-off such as Minecraft: Story Mode and Minecraft: Dungeons

  • 2013: Dota & LoL

    LOL and Dota 2 are MOBA (Massive Online Battle Arena) games released in 2009 and 2013. These two games are extremely as of 2019, with Dota 2 having 700k people playing consecutively every day. Strengthening the competitive E-sport scene, intense competition has made MOBA games one of the standout genres of eSports, with titles represented in some of the biggest tournaments (with the biggest prize pools) in the world. Professional teams from dozens of different countries compete in stadium-sized venues with tens of thousands of spectators in person and even more watching streams online.

  • 2017: PUBG & Fortnite

    In the early 2017, when PUBG was first released, it became one of the most populated game of all time with over 3 million consecutive players in January 2018, popularising the battle royale genre.
